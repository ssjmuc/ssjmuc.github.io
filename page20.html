Sacrament Preparation

To live, we must daily break the body and shed the blood of Creation. When we do this knowingly, lovingly, skillfully, reverently, it is a sacrament. When we do it ignorantly, greedily, clumsily, destructively, it is a desecration.

Baptism

At Baptism, parents accept the responsibility of training their children in the practice of the faith, teaching them to keep God’s commandments as taught by Jesus, and to love God and neighbor...

First Reconciliation and First Communion:

Our children learn by observing how the significant adults in their lives respond to their own Baptismal call. No classroom or textbook can provide this. It is important for a child’s remote preparation for sacraments to begin by observing his or her parents’/guardians’ behavior and priorities, which include coming to Sunday Mass and experiencing time for prayer and a growing relationship with God.  Parish families who wish to have their children continue their initiation into the Catholic Church are advised to enroll their children in

 Sacrament Preparation.

 It is expected to attend Mass regularly, by every family preparing their  children for First Communion. Children  generally receive immediate preparation for  First Reconciliation and First Communion  

Confirmation

It is the expectation of the parish that each young person wishing  to be confirmed has received ongoing religious instruction in  the grades. It is also expected that the young person attends Sunday Mass each week.
