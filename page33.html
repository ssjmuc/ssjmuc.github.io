Rebuilding Our Parish
Update for October 13th, 2017

Roof

The structural repair of the roof trusses has been completed in accordance with the engineerâ€™s specifications. The exterior shingles, many of which suffered damage during the fire, were removed and the new shingles were delivered Wednesday. Our expectation is that the roof will be completed before the end of the month. The debris trapped within the crevices of the Church ceiling has been removed.

Architects/Engineers

Foresight Architects have been working with the parish committee on seating plans and alternative liturgical furnishing layouts. Odea Lynch & Abbatista (OLA) has been retained to develop plans and specifications for the renovation of the heating and air conditioning systems. OLA will coordinate their efforts with Foresight Architects and Malanga Construction to make sure the various projects are sequenced properly. Paul Beck, the engineer who supervised the truss repair, has been retained to oversee the steeple replacement as well as the repair and renovation of the tower interior.

Progress Update

While the bulk of the plaster ceiling has maintained structural integrity, there are still areas of concern to be addressed. It is our intention to finalize the scope of work for the scraping, plastering and repainting of the Church. Foresight Architects will be presenting a proposal to install a full-height aluminum replacement steeple to the parish committee on October 25th. The committee will evaluate the proposal based on how closely the new steeple approximates the aesthetics of the original version as well as the cost. It is hoped that there will be considerable cost savings achieved by creating a more functional version of the internal structure of the tower, which would more than likely be a simple staircase with platforms at each landing.

Short and Long Term Goals

We anticipate formulating scopes of work for competitive bidding on the following: HVAC renovation and repair, tower reconstruction, exterior masonry repair and plastering and painting of Church interior. We are confident that bids will be received and vendors selected by sometime in December and that we will be able to confirm a timeline for execution and completion by January 1st. Our preliminary guess is that the actual work will take six to eight months from start to finish. We will continue to update this timeline, which is obviously a work in progress.
